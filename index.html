Express AR

Developed for EECS 440 - XR and Society at the University of Michigan.

Express AR is an augmented reality experience designed to teach emotional learning through interactive storytelling and facial expression recognition. Players take on the role of an actor guided by a virtual director, completing scene-based challenges by expressing emotions like joy, sadness, and surprise using only their face.

The game encourages emotional development in neurodivergent children using playful, camera-based AR interactions. Players earn in-game rewards and unlock accessories as they progress through acting challenges.

Technologies Used:
- Unity with AR Foundation: Built the AR experience with real-time face tracking and UI interactions.
- Barracuda & ONNX: Integrated emotion recognition using neural network inference directly in Unity.
- C# Scripting: Developed all core logic including level systems, emotion detection, and dynamic reward feedback.
- JIRA: Used for project management, tracking tasks, and organizing milestones.

P2: A2GO

Developed for EECS 440 - XR and Society at the University of Michigan.

An augmented reality (AR) experience designed to bring Ann Arbor to life through interactive location-based challenges. Players can customize their avatar and explore Ann Arbor while planting trees, battling squirrels, and collecting leaves!

Technologies Utilized:
- Unity (AR Foundation): Used as the primary game engine to develop the AR experience, integrating real-world mapping and interactions.
- Mapbox SDK: Implemented for real-time geolocation and navigation, allowing users to interact with specific Ann Arbor locations.
- C# Scripting: Developed core game mechanics, including challenge logic, reward systems, and real-world interactions.
- JIRA: Used for project management, tracking tasks, and organizing milestones.
